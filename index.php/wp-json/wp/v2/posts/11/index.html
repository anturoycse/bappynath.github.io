Taiwan wants to have \u201cmeaningful dialogue\u201d with China on an equal basis, president Tsai Ing-wen said on Saturday, extending an olive branch at a time of heightened military tension with Beijing, which claims the island as sovereign Chinese territory.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Democratic Taiwan has come under increasing pressure from Beijing, which has ramped up air force activity near the island in the past few weeks, including crossing the Taiwan Strait\u2019s sensitive mid line that normally serves as an unofficial buffer zone.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

As long as the Beijing authorities are willing to resolve antagonisms and improve cross-strait relations, while parity and dignity are maintained, we are willing to work together to facilitate meaningful dialogue<\/p><\/blockquote>\n\n\n\n

China says it is responding to \u201ccollusion\u201d between Washington and Taipei, angered at growing US support for the self-governed island. Beijing views this a precursor to Taiwan declaring formal independence, a red line for China.Advertisement<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Speaking at National Day celebrations, Tsai described the situation in the Taiwan Strait as \u201cquite tense.\u201d This, along with disputes in the South China Sea, a China-India border conflict and China\u2019s crackdown in Hong Kong, showed democracy and peace in the region were facing big challenges, she said.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

If Beijing can heed Taiwan\u2019s voice and jointly facilitate reconciliation and peaceful dialogue, regional tension can surely be resolved, she added.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cAs long as the Beijing authorities are willing to resolve antagonisms and improve cross-strait relations, while parity and dignity are maintained, we are willing to work together to facilitate meaningful dialogue,\u201d Tsai said.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Still, she has made strengthening Taiwan\u2019s armed forces a priority, and said she would keep pushing this, upholding the principle of neither seeking war nor fearing it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cOur commitment to our sovereignty and democratic values will not change, but we will also maintain strategic flexibility and be responsive to changes,\u201d she said, without elaborating.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
