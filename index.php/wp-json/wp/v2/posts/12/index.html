China and Sri Lanka plan to restart discussions on a free trade agreement, Sri Lankan president Gotabaya Rajapaksa said on Friday, following a high-level meeting between the two countries.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Seen as close to Beijing, Rajapaksa has appointed several members of his family to top cabinet positions since his party won a commanding majority in August\u2019s parliamentary elections – including his brother Mahinda, who previously also served as president.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Negotiations on a free trade agreement were last held in 2017.Advertisement<\/p>\n\n\n\n

China has been making increasing inroads into South Asia with its Belt and Road Initiative, aimed at financing critical infrastructure in dozens of countries across the world.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I want to prove that it is not the case and that this large-scale project will help improve the living standards of the people<\/p><\/blockquote>\n\n\n\n

Mahinda Rajapaksa<\/a><\/a><\/p>\n\n\n\n

In 2017, Sri Lanka signed over control of a Chinese-financed port and land around it to Beijing after incurring heavy losses, to the alarm of the United States and regional power India.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

On Friday, Rajapaksa rejected accusations that China wanted to trap Sri Lanka into mounting debt with the port deal.Advertisement<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cMany geo-political analysis interprets this project as a \u2018debt trap\u2019 set up by China to gain control over Sri Lankan affairs,\u201d Rajapaksa told the Chines delegation, led by Yang Jiechi, one of China\u2019s top diplomats.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cI want to prove that it is not the case and that this large-scale project will help improve the living standards of the people,\u201d he added.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
